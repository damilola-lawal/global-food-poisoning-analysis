  ## Key Insights
- India accounted for the highest number of reported cases across the 10-years period.
- Street food contributed the highest proportion of foodborne illness cases, indicating sanitation risks.
- Salmonella was the most common pathogen.
- Poor sanitation and lack of clean water were major root causes.

## Recommendations
- Strengthen hygiene monitoring for street vendors.
- Improve food safety regulation enforcement.
- Increase access to clean water and sanitation facilities.
- Implement public health awareness campaigns.

  ## Business/Public Health Impact
  The insights from this analysis can help public health agencies prioritize food safety monitoring, improve hygiene enforcement for street vendors, and allocate healthcare resources effectively.
